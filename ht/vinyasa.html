Vinyasa Yoga

Flow with breath and movement.

What is Vinyasa Yoga?

Vinyasa yoga is a dynamic style of yoga that focuses on linking breath with movement. Often referred to as "flow" yoga, it creates a rhythmic sequence of poses that transitions smoothly from one to the next. It builds strength, flexibility, and mindfulness.

Common Vinyasa Flow Sequence

1. Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

Begin standing tall with feet together, grounding yourself and focusing on your breath.

2. Forward Fold (Uttanasana)

Exhale and fold forward from your hips, keeping a slight bend in your knees if needed.

3. Plank Pose

Step back into a plank position, engaging your core and aligning your body.

4. Chaturanga Dandasana

Lower down to a low plank, keeping elbows close to your sides.

5. Upward Facing Dog (Urdhva Mukha Svanasana)

Lift your chest forward and up, straightening your arms and keeping your thighs off the ground.

6. Downward Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

Lift your hips and form an inverted "V" shape, stretching through your back and legs.
