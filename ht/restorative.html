Restorative Yoga

Relax, rejuvenate, and restore with gentle poses.

What is Restorative Yoga?

Restorative yoga is a gentle practice that focuses on relaxation and healing. Using props like bolsters, blocks, and blankets, poses are held for longer durations, allowing the body and mind to fully relax and restore.

Relaxing Poses for Restorative Yoga

1. Child's Pose (Balasana)

Kneel on the mat, sit back on your heels, and stretch your arms forward. Let your forehead rest on the mat or a bolster.

2. Supported Bridge Pose (Setu Bandha Sarvangasana)

Lie on your back and place a yoga block under your sacrum for support. Relax your arms at your sides.

3. Reclined Butterfly Pose (Supta Baddha Konasana)

Lie on your back, bring the soles of your feet together, and let your knees fall outward. Use props for support.

4. Legs-Up-the-Wall Pose (Viparita Karani)

Sit sideways against a wall, then lie back and extend your legs up the wall. Rest your arms comfortably by your sides.
